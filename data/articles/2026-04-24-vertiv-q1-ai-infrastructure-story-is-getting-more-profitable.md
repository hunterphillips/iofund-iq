## Thesis
Vertiv's Q1 results demonstrate accelerating operating leverage as AI infrastructure demand scales, with margin outperformance the standout story. Revenue met expectations but Q2 guidance was soft; management offset this with a raised full-year outlook, signaling strong confidence in a 2H acceleration. Prefabrication, bring-your-own-power, and liquid cooling expansions are broadening Vertiv's addressable content per data center.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $2.65B, +30% YoY, +23% organic; in line with consensus
- Q2 revenue guide midpoint: $3.35B, slightly below $3.4B estimate; implies +27% YoY
- FY26 revenue guide raised $250M to $13.5–14B midpoint (~34% YoY, ~30% organic)
- 2H implied growth: ~40% YoY to hit midpoint
- Adjusted operating margin: 20.8%, beat 19% guide, +3.7 pts YoY
- FY26 adjusted operating margin raised 0.8 pts to 23.3%
- Adjusted EPS: $1.17, beat $1.01 estimate, +83% YoY; FY guide raised to $6.35 midpoint (+51% YoY)
- Adjusted FCF: $652.8M, +147% YoY; FY target $2.2B
- Inventories: +26% QoQ to $1.83B; deferred revenue +35.6% QoQ
- Americas organic growth: +44.3%; EMEA organic: -29.4%; APAC organic: +12%
- Net leverage improved from 0.5x to 0.2x QoQ

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Sequential inventory and deferred revenue surges are leading indicators of 2H revenue conversion and potential 2027 strength.
- Prefabrication (OneCore, SmartRun) and BYOP initiatives expand Vertiv's total system revenue capture beyond component supply.
- Successive quarterly margin raises suggest FY exit GAAP operating margin could reach 22–23%, implying 4–5 pts YoY expansion.
- EMEA described as a "coiled spring" with return to growth expected in 2H; APAC guided to accelerate to mid-20s organic.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Q2 margin dip expected as new capacity comes online and tariff offsets (including Section 232 changes) are worked through.
- Deal cycles described as "elongated" to 12–18 months, introducing timing risk to 2H conversion assumptions.
- Valuation trades above 3-year median; I/O Fund flags technicals as primary risk management tool given premium pricing.
- Americas growth expected to moderate to high-30s by year-end, increasing reliance on EMEA/APAC recovery materializing.
