## Thesis
Agentic AI is structurally shifting data center spending toward CPUs, which handle orchestration while GPUs handle inference. The CPU-to-GPU ratio in AI clusters is expected to move from 1:4–1:8 today toward 1:1–1:2, driving a server CPU TAM that AMD now estimates at $120B by 2030 (35% CAGR), up from an 18% CAGR forecast just seven months prior.

## Key numbers
- Server CPU TAM by 2030: $120B+ (AMD), $132B (Citi), $170B (UBS), $125B (BofA)
- Agentic CPU segment CAGR 2025–2030: ~185% (Citi), reaching $59B
- AMD server CPU revenue growth: +50% YoY in Q1 2026; guiding +70% YoY in Q2
- AMD x86 revenue share: 46.2% in Q1 2026, up from 25.1% in Q2 2023
- Nvidia standalone Vera CPU revenue visibility: ~$20B in fiscal 2026
- Intel CPU order fulfillment: ~40% of yearly backlog; lead times 8–22 weeks domestically, up to 8 months in Asia
- AMD EPYC effectively sold out in 2026; delivery windows >30 weeks
- CPU price increases: 10–35% QoQ (Intel/AMD per Reuters); up to 3x in won terms (The Elec)
- Goldman Sachs token consumption forecast: 24x increase to 120 quadrillion/month by 2030, with agents >80% of total
- Arm AGI CPU performance per watt: ~2x vs. x86 (internal estimates)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- CPU demand is supply-constrained now, giving Intel and AMD near-term pricing power; ASPs could become a larger growth driver alongside units.
- Standalone CPU racks (Nvidia Vera, Arm AGI, Intel rack blueprints) represent an architectural shift allowing CPU scaling independent of GPU additions—expanding the addressable market significantly.
- AMD is the most direct beneficiary: targeting >50% x86 server share by 2030, with Venice (2nm TSMC) ramping and Verano (AI-native) launching in 2027.
- Intel's Diamond Rapids (Xeon 7) delay from H2 2026 to mid-2027 gives AMD a meaningful competitive window.
- Nvidia enters aggressively with $20B CPU revenue guided for this year, framing CPUs as additive to GPUs rather than cannibalistic.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Intel Diamond Rapids delay could extend further, but Intel's 18A node offers competitive power efficiency if it executes.
- Arm's AGI CPU (co-developed with Meta) threatens x86 share with 2x performance-per-watt advantage; hyperscalers may accelerate in-house Arm designs.
- ByteDance developing in-house CPUs signals that prolonged shortages could accelerate customer self-sufficiency, capping long-term vendor pricing power.
- AMD's $60B server CPU revenue target by 2030 requires ~5x growth from current ~$12B run rate—ambitious even in a rapidly expanding TAM.
