## Thesis
Core Scientific is transitioning from Bitcoin mining to greenfield colocation data center development, with revenue beginning to inflect as CoreWeave capacity ramps. The 4.5 GW gross power pipeline offers substantial long-term revenue potential (~$4.2–5.1B annually at full build-out), but execution risk is high given heavy debt reliance, no second major customer signed yet, and a failed hyperscaler exclusivity deal.

## Key numbers
- 243 MW billable in Q1 → ~$350M annualized colocation revenue; targeting 450 MW by end of Q2
- Full CoreWeave contract: 590 MW, targeting completion early 2027; ~$850M annualized revenue
- Gross power pipeline: 4.5 GW (excl. CoreWeave's 590 MW); 3 GW leasable, 1.5 GW grid-connected
- Pipeline revenue potential: $4.2–5.1B/year at $1.4–1.7M per MW per year
- Full 3 GW build-out cost estimate: ~$33B (~$11M/leasable MW); requires ~$6–6.6B cash + ~$25B project financing
- $3.3B senior secured notes (7.75% rate) raised; ~$256M annual interest burden
- Q1 revenue: $115.2M, +44.9% YoY; colocation revenue $77.5M, +804% YoY
- Cash gross profit margin target raised 500 bps to 82.5% midpoint on CoreWeave contract
- FY26 revenue estimate: $622M (+95% YoY); FY27: $1.04B (+66.5% YoY)
- Cash: $1.0B; debt: $2.1B (pre-notes); stockholders' deficit: ~$1.3B

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Revenue inflection is real but almost entirely CoreWeave-dependent; diversification is the critical next catalyst.
- Greenfield strategy improves delivery predictability (18-month RFS) but requires self-funding pre-contract, with no hard capex guardrail in place.
- Behind-the-meter power for Pecos and Muskogee expansions could add $1B+ in costs and is not yet secured.
- GAAP EPS could turn positive as early as Q3 2026, assuming no further impairment charges.
- Classified as an Advanced-only momentum play; I/O Fund does not hold shares at time of writing.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Hyperscaler deal fell through under exclusivity; three new engagements are early-stage with no timeline given—prolonged delay increases pre-contract capex burn.
- Junk-rated debt (7.75%) adds $256M/year in interest; balance sheet deterioration if new contracts lag construction spend.
- Bitcoin mining wind-down in 2H 2026 may trigger additional impairment charges, masking underlying colocation progress.
- Behind-the-meter power not secured for GW-scale site expansions; grid load studies still pending.
