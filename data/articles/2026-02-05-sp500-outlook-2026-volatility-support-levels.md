## Thesis
The 2025 recovery was a historically rare event matching only 1980 and 1999 analogs, and the dominant 26-year Gann cycle now points toward a notable correction beginning in Q1–Q2 2026. Multiple leading risk assets (Mag 7, Bitcoin, high-beta, financials) have already topped out while the S&P 500 grinds higher on extreme sentiment, suggesting the broad market is tracing the final swings of an extended fifth wave.

## Key numbers
- 6 of 7 Mag 7 stocks are >15% below all-time highs on average; collectively ~28% of S&P 500 weighting
- Bitcoin exited at avg cost basis ~$105,017 (~90% of position); BTC down >40% from its October 2025 top
- NAAIM exposure readings: 78th–96th percentile for 3+ consecutive months
- S&P 500 support zone: 6,780–6,720 (must hold for uptrend to remain intact)
- Upside target if support holds: 7,132–7,375
- SMH support: $383–$367; upside target $440–$513
- Current rally: ~45% off April 2025 low over ~10 months (matches 1999 analog's ~53% over 10 months)
- Margin debt at record highs, exceeding 2021 peak
- 8 major cycles tracked: only 3 of 8 suggest 2026 finishes higher

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- No major support has broken yet; market can continue grinding higher into late February/March before a more significant pullback.
- Sector rotation from tech/high-beta toward broader participation (energy, biotech, gold, materials) is currently constructive but watch for rotation into defensives as a warning sign.
- I/O Fund is positioned increasingly defensively; key trigger is a break below 6,780 on the S&P 500.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Break of 6,780–6,720 S&P 500 support would materially narrow viable upside paths and signal a larger drawdown.
- Record margin debt amplifies downside velocity if sentiment shifts; crowded positioning leaves little buying power to sustain the rally.
- Bitcoin's volume/RSI deterioration mirrors late-2021 pattern, which preceded a broad market top by ~2 months—worth monitoring as a leading indicator.
