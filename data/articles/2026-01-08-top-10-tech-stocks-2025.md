## Thesis
Despite DeepSeek fears, tariffs, and AI-bubble skepticism, AI infrastructure demand drove exceptional returns across memory, storage, power, and software in 2025. The Nasdaq-100 gained 20.2% and S&P 500 gained 16.4%, but the top AI-linked names vastly outperformed. The structural pattern—revenue acceleration plus operating leverage converting scarcity into margin expansion—is the blueprint I/O Fund is applying to 2026.

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- AI demand has structurally shifted toward physical bottlenecks—memory (HBM), storage (NAND/HDD), and power—not just software.
- HBM scales faster than GPUs per system; each GPU generation (Hopper→Blackwell→Rubin) requires more memory, creating a compounding multiplier effect favoring MU and similar suppliers.
- S&P 500 / Nasdaq-100 index inclusions (SNDK, WDC, HOOD, APPS) provided meaningful secondary price catalysts on top of fundamental improvements.
- Revenue acceleration combined with operating leverage (not just growth alone) was the common thread among all top performers.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Memory and storage names (SNDK, WDC, MU) are exposed to NAND/HDD pricing cycles; margin expansion assumed continued supply-demand tightness.
- Two of the top 10 stocks (#9 optical interconnect, #10 AI ad-tech) are paywalled—incomplete picture for replication.
- Macro headwinds (tariffs, geopolitical tensions) persisted throughout 2025 and could intensify, pressuring hyperscaler capex commitments.
- GEV's order book visibility extends to 2028, but execution risk on capacity expansion and the $5.275B Prolec GE acquisition closing mid-2026 warrants monitoring.
