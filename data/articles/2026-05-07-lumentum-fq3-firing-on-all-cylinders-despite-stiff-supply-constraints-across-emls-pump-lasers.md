## Thesis
Lumentum is in a demand-constrained growth phase where supply shortfalls across EMLs, pump lasers, and transceivers are the primary limiter—not demand. Revenue growth is reaccelerating sharply with margins expanding rapidly, and the upcoming OCS/CPO ramps represent a multi-billion-dollar opportunity that could dwarf current revenue levels by 2028.

## Key numbers
- FQ3 revenue: $808.4M, +90% YoY, +21.5% QoQ
- FQ4 guide: $960M–$1.01B midpoint, implying ~105% YoY and ~22% QoQ growth
- GAAP gross margin: 44.2% (+15 pts YoY); adjusted gross margin: 47.9% (+12.7 pts YoY)
- GAAP operating margin: 21.6% (+30 pts YoY); adjusted: 32.2% (+21.4 pts YoY)
- Adjusted EPS: $2.37 (+316% YoY); FQ4 guide $2.85–$3.05 (+235% YoY at midpoint)
- Systems segment: $275.1M (+121% YoY, +24% QoQ); cloud transceivers +40% QoQ
- Narrow linewidth lasers: +120% YoY (9th consecutive growth quarter); pump lasers: +80% YoY
- 200G EML revenue: more than doubled QoQ
- EML/transceiver supply-demand imbalance: >30% (widened from 25–30% last quarter)
- Nvidia invested $2B in March; cash/short-term investments now $3.17B vs. $3.28B convertible notes
- Scale-up CPO incremental revenue capacity from Greensboro fab: >$5B annual run rate (contributions from 2028)
- $2B/quarter revenue target: consensus expects December 2027

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Supply constraints are widening, not easing—this preserves pricing power and suggests sequential margin expansion as new capacity comes online in 2H 2026.
- Pump laser tightness emerged as an unexpected bottleneck this quarter; near-term relief is largely within Lumentum's control given secured InP substrate inventory.
- CW laser insourcing began a quarter early and should lift transceiver margins as 1.6T ramps in FQ4, where adjusted operating margin is guided to ~35.5%.
- OCS ramp on track for $400M in calendar 2H 2026, scaling to >$1B in calendar 2027; TPU v8 could add incremental upside.
- Scale-up CPO is characterized as potentially Lumentum's largest single opportunity, with first shipments in late 2027 and material revenue in 2028.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Heavy capex requirements to expand EML and pump laser capacity could pressure free cash flow (FCF was only $79M or ~10% of revenue in FQ3).
- OCS supply chain tightness could create lumpiness in the ramp; dependent partly on Google TPU v8 timing.
- Transceiver margins still trail peers; execution on 1.6T yield improvement and CW insourcing is critical.
- Scale-up CPO is 6–7 quarters away—a long runway where competitive dynamics or hyperscaler priorities could shift.
