## Thesis
Since its May 2020 inception, the I/O Fund has compounded at 29.2% annualized, reaching 326% cumulative return—outpacing the Nasdaq-100 by 152% and institutional tech portfolios by 294%. The 2025 outperformance was driven by concentrated AI semiconductor and power-infrastructure positions entered tactically below year-open prices, combined with aggressive crypto reduction.

## Key numbers
- 2025 total portfolio return: 37% (vs. Nasdaq-100 +20%, S&P 500 +16%)
- 2025 equity-only return: 56% (ranked ~3rd among U.S. equity portfolios)
- Cumulative return since May 2020: 326%
- Annualized return since inception: 29.2%
- Positions beating Nasdaq-100: 11 in 2025 (up from 10 in 2024, 7 in 2023)
- Bloom Energy average return: 305% (best single entry: 422%)
- AI networking stock: ~295% average return before trimming; stock later fell ~50%
- AI semiconductor stock: 140% average return across 5 buys (Feb–Apr 2025)
- Bitcoin miner: 93% average gain
- AI data/software platform: 79% average return
- AI advertising/social media firm: 51% return
- Nvidia buys: ~$94.48 (Apr 4) and ~$87.99 (Apr 7) after selling half at avg cost $130.88
- Bitcoin trimmed from 10% → 1% allocation, locking gains between $85K–$113K

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Tactical entries below January 1 opening prices were the primary alpha driver; timing around known buy-target zones (e.g., Nvidia $80–$90) was central to the strategy.
- Crypto drag was significant—Bitcoin down 5%, altcoins down up to 40%—reinforcing the value of reducing crypto exposure early using technical/liquidity signals.
- Bloom Energy and lesser-known AI semiconductors/networking names contributed more to 2025 returns than Nvidia alone.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Crypto allocation remains a performance wildcard; even a 1% residual position adds volatility.
- Concentrated positions (up to 15% in Bloom, 11% in AI networking) amplify both upside and drawdown risk—the networking stock fell ~50% after the trim.
- Past audited returns do not guarantee future results; the strategy's edge depends on continued early identification of AI infrastructure winners in a rapidly rotating market.
