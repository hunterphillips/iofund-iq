## Thesis
Nvidia's path to a $20 trillion market cap by decade-end is strengthening, driven not by GPU unit growth alone but by a shift toward token economics and system-level value. The Groq LPX acquisition and inference-optimized rack architecture represent an underpriced catalyst, while Wall Street has historically underestimated Nvidia's revenue by 2–4x on a 1–5 year horizon.

## Key numbers
- Blackwell revenue (2025, compute + networking): ~$184B
- Expected data center revenue (CY2026): ~$320B
- Jensen Huang's cumulative Blackwell + Rubin target through 2027: ~$1T (implying ~$500B in CY2027, ~54% YoY growth)
- Street consensus FY2028 (Jan): ~$480B vs. $138B estimated three quarters post-May 2023 breakout (~3.5x revision)
- FY2031 consensus: ~$758B vs. $208B from same period (~4x revision); vs. $343B just one year ago (~2x revision in 12 months)
- Analyst base case FY2031: $758B; I/O Fund base case: ~$930B–$1T (23% gap)
- $20T market cap assumption: ~$930B data center revenue at ~22x P/S (vs. 3-year median ~28x)
- Custom silicon AI accelerator share: 20.9% (2025) → projected 27.8% (2026, TrendForce)
- Groq 3 LPX throughput claim: up to 35x higher throughput per megawatt vs. prior gen
- Token throughput target: 100 TPS → 1,500+ TPS (Rubin + Groq combined)
- Groq rack addressable opportunity per Huang: ~$300B annual revenue; up to 25% of data center footprint
- Networking growth last reported quarter: +263% YoY

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- The $1T cumulative revenue comment from GTC was already priced in; it offered little alpha. The real catalyst is the inference/token-economics story, which the market has not yet fully modeled.
- Analyst estimate revisions are the primary stock price driver; the pattern of persistent 2–4x underestimation suggests the inference opportunity is similarly mispriced today.
- Nvidia is transitioning its value proposition from raw compute benchmarks to cost-per-token and tokens-per-watt at the system level—a framework that could sustain premium valuation even as GPU market share erodes.
- The $10T market cap thesis is now consensus (Jensen confirmed it publicly); the differentiated call is $20T by ~2030.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Custom silicon (Google TPUs, etc.) gaining accelerator share (20.9% → 27.8% in one year); AMD MI400 Helios launch in H2 2026 adds further pressure.
- Nvidia's P/S is trading well below its 3-year median (~28x) while peers Broadcom and AMD trade at or above theirs—buyers not yet stepping in despite apparent discount.
- Technical support: stock broke minor support at $176; key floor at $170. Break below opens $155–$135 range. Needs reclaim of $200 to signal next leg higher.
- Groq integration claims (35x throughput, $300B opportunity) are forward-looking and depend on execution, buyer appetite, and whether premium inference pricing (~$150/million tokens) finds market acceptance.
