## Thesis
I/O Fund's 2025 research consistently front-ran major market inflection points—flagging the April AI correction in February, buying Nvidia near its lows, and arguing that AI monetization is accelerating rather than topping. The firm believes the AI revenue cycle is just beginning, with Big Tech capex growth and custom silicon demand as the primary evidence.

## Key numbers
- NVDA drawdown after February warning: ~25%; SMH -22%, QQQ -17%
- NVDA bought at $87.99 (April 7); that tranche returned ~92% since entry
- ALAB accumulated at avg cost basis ~$69.42; returned ~140% in 2025; April-to-present gain ~269% vs. 27% YTD if held all year
- AMD outperformed NVDA by ~37% following I/O Fund's June analysis (AMD +68% vs. NVDA +31%)
- Big Tech Q3 CapEx: +19% QoQ, +75% YoY — strongest of the year
- Nvidia data center segment needs ~36% CAGR through 2030 to support a $20T market cap target
- I/O Fund 5-year cumulative return: ~210% (claimed #5 among hedge funds, #2 among ETFs)
- Micron cited as up ~6x relative to Nvidia in a specific period; +120% YTD at time of writing

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- The firm views AI monetization as not yet fully underway, pushing back hard on dot-com bubble comparisons.
- Meta is characterized as potentially the #2 AI revenue generator behind Nvidia, surpassing Microsoft.
- Broadcom's custom XPU business is positioned as the key inference-era beneficiary offering cost/energy advantages over GPUs.
- Technical layering into positions during drawdowns (e.g., ALAB, NVDA) drove outsized returns vs. buy-and-hold.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Nvidia faces ongoing China export restrictions and generational GPU transition delays (GB200 timing slippage).
- Reddit's growth is partly Google-search-algorithm-dependent, making it vulnerable to AI search disruption.
- Crypto (Bitcoin) flagged as having peaked in Q3 2025; soft price action expected to persist.
- High-profile analysts argue Big Tech capex is a one-time event—if correct, the AI infrastructure thesis weakens materially.
