## Thesis
Agentic AI is rapidly pushing CPU-to-GPU ratios from 1:4–1:8 toward 1:1 or better, dramatically expanding Arm's addressable market. The AGI CPU entering TSMC production with new customers (Oracle, ByteDance) joining Meta, OpenAI, and Cloudflare signals accelerating demand, while Arm's CEO hinted core-per-GW growth could exceed the previously stated 4× figure. Royalty tailwinds from v9/CSS architecture upgrades and a doubling of new Arm-based data center chips in 2027 support a multi-year growth re-rating.

## Key numbers
- Stock rally: ~100% in roughly one month post-earnings
- CPU:GPU ratio shift: from 1:4–1:8 today toward 1:1 or better (Intel/AMD confirmation)
- Agentic ecosystem growth: ~5× in three months (GitHub stars metric)
- CPU core demand per GW: CEO guided 4×, then suggested 8×–10× is plausible
- Server CPU TAM: Arm's $100B forecast; AMD recently doubled its estimate to $120B
- Arm's AGI CPU revenue target: $15B by FY31
- Estimated FY26 server CPU royalty revenue: ~$220M (~8–9% of Cloud AI royalties)
- Projected server CPU royalties by 2028: $880M (2× core growth + 2× royalty rates) to $1.76B (4× core growth)
- Google TPU v8 shipments: ~4.13M in 2026, rising ~139% to ~9.87M in 2027 (UBS)
- Implied Axion CPU demand from Google TPUs: ~4M units in 2027
- Amazon/Meta deal: 30–50M Graviton cores (~156K–260K chips) for ≥3 years
- Nvidia Vera CPU TAM: $200B; $20B revenue visibility this year
- Mizuho price target progression: $290 → $360 → $425 → $500
- Wells Fargo price target: raised from $255 to $410

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- CSS wins are increasingly critical; subsequent-gen CSS carries ~3× higher royalty rates vs. first-gen v9, making each design win materially more valuable.
- Eight new Arm-based data center chips expected in 2027 (vs. three in 2026), with four featuring substantially higher core counts—direct royalty growth catalyst.
- Near-term (1H FY27) revenue growth is soft (~18–20% YoY), but the setup for a significant re-acceleration in 2H and into 2028 is building.
- Nvidia's RTX Spark (Arm-based PC superchip) could offset PC-segment weakness tied to elevated memory costs.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Supply constraints at TSMC remain the primary bottleneck limiting AGI CPU revenue recognition; Arm provided little clarity on supply ramp timing at Computex.
- Near-term deceleration: FQ2 growth expected to dip to ~18% YoY before rebounding—sentiment could soften if macro or hyperscaler capex shifts.
- CPU:GPU ratio assumptions are speculative; if agentic AI deployments scale more slowly or favor alternative architectures, TAM expansion could lag projections.
- Valuation risk: shares already up ~100% in a month; multiple expansion is priced for an aggressive ramp scenario.
