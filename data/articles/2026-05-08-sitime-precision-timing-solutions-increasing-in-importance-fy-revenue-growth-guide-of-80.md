## Thesis
SiTime is benefiting from a structural shift in AI data centers where inference workloads, faster networking speeds (1.6T), and co-packaged optics demand 2–4x more precision timing content per system than prior training deployments. The Renesas timing unit acquisition nearly doubles CED revenue and expands the clock IC portfolio 10x, positioning SiTime toward a $1B revenue run rate. Growth is re-accelerating with improving margins and a healthy supply chain.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $113.6M (+88% YoY), beat consensus by ~10%
- Q1 adjusted EPS: $1.44, +454% YoY, beat by ~21%
- Q1 adjusted gross margin: 64.5%, +7 pts YoY
- Q1 adjusted operating margin: 28%, +25 pts YoY
- Q1 operating cash flow: $31.2M, +108% YoY
- CED segment: $75.7M, +158% YoY, +17% QoQ; now 67% of revenue
- Q2 revenue guide: $145M midpoint, ~109% YoY growth; beat estimates by ~29%
- FY2026 revenue growth guide: ≥80% (vs. prior LT target of 25–30%)
- Renesas timing unit: ~$1.5B acquisition price; expected >$300M revenue in first 12 months post-close, ~75% from CED; ~70% adjusted gross margins
- Clock IC funnel: ~$300M (as of Q3), revenue sub-$20M at that time
- CPO switches: 3x higher timing content vs. standard switches
- Inference rack content: potentially $1,000+ vs. "multiple hundreds" for training

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Inference deployments are the primary demand driver; GPU utilization needs to rise from 20–40% toward 50–60%, and SiTime's synchronization products are directly enabling that improvement.
- 1.6T optical module ramp in 2026 carries higher ASPs than 800G; 400G/800G volumes remain strong for at least two more years, providing a durable revenue base.
- Book-to-bill exceeded 1.5x in Q4 and is reportedly moving higher in Q1, with orders weighted to H1 but extending into H2—supporting confidence in the full-year guide.
- Renesas acquisition is margin-accretive (~70% gross margin vs. SiTime's ~61% pre-deal) and adds 10 hyperscalers and 7 AI server customers with minimal product overlap, opening cross-sell opportunities.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Consumer/IoT segment is soft (-1% YoY, -31% QoQ) and expected to be a larger revenue mix in H2, which could modestly pressure blended gross margins.
- Renesas timing unit integration risk; deal close timing affects when the $300M+ revenue contribution begins flowing.
- Inference GPU utilization improvement is a customer-dependent variable; slower ramp could soften demand signals.
- High stock-based compensation (27% of revenue in Q1) creates a wide GAAP vs. non-GAAP margin gap to monitor.
