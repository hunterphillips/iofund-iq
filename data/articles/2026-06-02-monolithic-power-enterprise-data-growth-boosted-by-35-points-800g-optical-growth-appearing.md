## Thesis
MPS is benefiting from two converging AI infrastructure tailwinds: vertical power delivery (VPD) adoption driven by next-gen GPUs exceeding 2,000W, and an unexpected surge in 800G optical module demand lifting the Communications segment. Management's FY26 Enterprise Data growth floor was raised 35 points to 85% YoY in a single quarter, signaling accelerating order visibility and no supply constraints.

## Key numbers
- Enterprise Data Q1 revenue: $262.8M, +97.7% YoY, +12.6% QoQ
- FY26 Enterprise Data growth floor raised: 50% → 85% YoY (~$250M raise vs. prior guide; implies ~$1.3B)
- Communications Q1 revenue: $111.5M, +55.5% YoY, +33.1% QoQ (fastest sequential since Q3 2024)
- Q1 total revenue: $804.2M, +26.1% YoY; Q2 guide midpoint ~$900M, +35.4% YoY
- Capacity target raised: $4B → $6B (+50%); FY26 consensus revenue ~$3.7B (within 10% of old cap)
- Capex: record $70.9M in Q1
- Adjusted EPS FY26 forecast: ~$24, +35% YoY
- >800G optical transceiver shipments projected +2.6X in 2026 (TrendForce: ~63M units)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- VPD is becoming mandatory for Rubin/MI450X-class GPUs; MPS's monolithic single-silicon approach and integration flexibility are structural competitive advantages over multi-chip rivals.
- Communications segment could rival Enterprise Data as a growth driver; Goldman Sachs projects optical-to-GPU attach ratios rising 2–3x with Rubin, extending the optics tailwind into 2027.
- Supply is not a constraint; management explicitly stated ordering strength, not supply limits, governs the growth floor, leaving room for further upside raises in Q2/Q3.
- Capacity expansion to $6B is urgent given FY26 estimates are already near the old $4B ceiling.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Gross margin headwinds flagged for 2H 2026; stuck near 55.5% for four consecutive quarters with rising input costs.
- 2H order visibility remains limited; Rubin ramp timing (initial Q3, accelerating Q4) is the key swing factor for whether the 85% floor moves higher again.
- MPS is pursuing SiC-based 800V solutions while Nvidia may prefer GaN; technology mismatch could limit participation in the 800V rack architecture shift (revenue not expected until 2027–2028 regardless).
- Inventory jumped ~10% QoQ and receivables ~18% QoQ—worth monitoring for demand pull-forward signals.
