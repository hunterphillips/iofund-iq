## Thesis
CoreWeave beat Q1 revenue estimates but guided Q2 roughly 6.5% below consensus, while maintaining a $12–13B full-year target that implies a dramatic 2H acceleration. Margins contracted sharply in Q1 and the path to profitability has deteriorated significantly, with the core tension being that capex (~$2.60 spent per $1 of revenue) and surging interest payments are outpacing revenue growth. The stock is best suited to a lower-rate, risk-on macro environment.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $2.08B, +112% YoY, +32% QoQ; beat estimates by ~5.5%
- Q2 revenue guide: $2.45–2.6B midpoint (~6.5% below consensus $2.7B)
- Full-year 2026 revenue guide: $12–13B; implies ~$7.9B in 2H
- Backlog: $99.4B, +284% YoY, +49% QoQ; ~36% (~$35.8B) to be recognized in next 24 months
- FY26 capex guide: $31–35B vs. ~$12.5B revenue midpoint
- Annualized interest payments: >$2B in Q1, guided to ~$2.8B annualized in Q2
- Cash: $2.27B; debt: $24.86B (debt ~11x cash)
- Q1 adjusted operating margin: 1%, down 16 pts YoY; Q4 target: low double digits
- Q1 adjusted EBITDA margin: 56%, down ~6 pts YoY
- FY26 adjusted EPS estimate: ($3.29), deteriorated from ($0.23) in November
- Meta + OpenAI: ~65% of Q1 revenue; combined contractual obligations ~$57.6B
- Exit 2026 ARR target: $18–19B; exit 2027 ARR: $30B (75% already contracted)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Revenue and margin inflection are both back-half-loaded; 2H adjusted operating income must be >11x 1H to hit guidance—execution risk is high.
- Backlog growth and ARR targets roughly match consensus, meaning upside requires faster-than-guided capacity additions, which demands even more capex.
- The Omni/SaaS pivot faces a structural mismatch: ~65% of revenue comes from hyperscalers (Meta, OpenAI) who have little need for CoreWeave's higher-margin software add-ons.
- Lease-heavy power strategy saves ~86% on facility capex vs. greenfield, preserving dollars for GPU procurement, but introduces third-party execution risk.
- I/O Fund removed CRWV from its Top 15 list due to circular investing dynamics (Nvidia is simultaneously supplier, investor, and customer; Jane Street is both customer and lender).

## Risks / watch-fors
- Interest payments rising faster than revenue (~38% QoQ in Q1); nearly 90% of debt carries 6–15% rates, none maturing before 2029.
- Component shortages (memory, storage, labor) increasingly constrain capacity ramp beyond just power availability.
- ARR exit targets for both 2026 and 2027 imply Q4 revenue slightly below current consensus, putting pressure on accelerating buildout and thus capex.
- Macro sensitivity: as a long-duration, heavy-debt cash-burn story, the stock is highly vulnerable to rising interest rates.
