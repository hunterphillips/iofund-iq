## Thesis
Lumentum (LITE) is positioned as the dominant supplier of indium phosphide-based optical components underpinning Nvidia's multi-year AI networking roadmap. The firm entered the position in January 2026 before Nvidia's $2B investment was announced, and the stock has since returned ~135%, roughly 6.8x Nvidia's ~20% gain over the same window. The CPO ramp, OCS contracts, and capacity expansion create a multi-year compounding opportunity that has barely begun.

## Key numbers
- LITE YTD return: 135.4% vs. NVDA 19.9% (same window)
- Q3 FY2026 revenue: $808M, +90% YoY (fastest in company history)
- Q4 FY2026 guidance midpoint: $980M, implying ~105% YoY growth
- Adjusted gross margin Q3: 47.9% (+12.7pp YoY); operating margin: 32.2% (+21.4pp YoY)
- Adjusted net income: $226M, +185% YoY
- Currently under-shipping demand by >30% despite leading global InP capacity
- InP capacity expansion: +50% from Dec 2025 to Dec 2026
- OCS backlog: $400M; OCS revenue target >$1B in calendar 2027
- CPO Phase 0 revenue target: $100M in Q4 calendar 2026; Phase 1 is 3–4x larger; Phase 2 is 10x larger
- Greensboro fab target: $5B incremental annual revenue capacity (vs. ~$2.5B LTM revenue)
- AI optical transceiver market: $16.5B (2025) → $26B (2026), +58% YoY (TrendForce)
- Optical AI TAM: $18B (2025) → $90B (2030), ~5x expansion
- FY2027 forward P/E: ~49x (vs. ~23x historical avg); FY2028 P/E drops to ~32x on $28 NTM EPS

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Nvidia sole-sourced Lumentum for UHP lasers in CPO switches; UHP revenue has not yet meaningfully started, meaning current strong results are driven entirely by transceivers and components.
- Capacity is the binding constraint across EMLs, pump lasers, narrow linewidth lasers, and transceivers—all effectively sold out, giving Lumentum pricing leverage with non-Nvidia buyers.
- Greensboro fab (mid-2028 ramp) is the "moonshot," potentially tripling revenue with semiconductor-like margins.
- Valuation looks stretched on near-term multiples but normalizes significantly by FY2028 before Phase 2 CPO inflects.

## Risks / watch-fors
- InP substrate supply chain: China export controls and US tariffs on Chinese substrates are a key risk; CEO flagged substrates as top concern, though a 7-year non-Chinese supply deal partially mitigates this.
- Nvidia concentration: much of Lumentum's capacity is being absorbed by a single customer; any Nvidia architectural shift or demand slowdown would have outsized impact.
- Valuation premium: trading ~109% above historical forward P/E average; execution on CPO and OCS ramps must materialize on schedule to justify current price.
- Supply gap widening: as UHP scales, the demand/supply imbalance is expected to grow, which helps margins but caps unit volume growth until Greensboro comes online in mid-2028.
