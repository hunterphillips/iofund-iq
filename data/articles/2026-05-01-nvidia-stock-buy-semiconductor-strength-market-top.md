## Thesis
I/O Fund is significantly trimming its long-held Nvidia position as the AI hardware cycle shifts toward inference and ASIC-based servers, eroding Nvidia's moat and margin durability. Technically, the semiconductor sector's surge looks like a fifth-wave exhaustion move—a potential bull trap—unsupported by confirmation from financials, industrials, and the equal-weight Mag 7. A meaningful correction is likely before the longer-term uptrend resumes.

## Key numbers
- ASIC-based AI server shipments rising to ~28% of mix in 2026, up from a smaller base (TrendForce)
- GPU-based AI servers still ~70% of 2026 shipments but share declining
- SMH up ~470% from Oct 2022 low; NVDA up ~1,700% same period
- SMH currently >30% above its late-Oct 2025 top; NVDA still ~1% below that same level
- XLF ~8% below its Jan 2026 top; XLI in similar corrective structure; XLF downside target ~$44 (~14% lower)
- Rubin platform reportedly delayed ~one quarter
- I/O Fund up ~35% YTD vs. Nasdaq-100 up ~9% YTD

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- The Nvidia/SMH divergence (SMH at new highs, NVDA lagging) has historically preceded sector corrections every time it appeared since May 2023.
- Elliott Wave analysis flags SMH, NVDA, AVGO, and TSM all in low-volume, low-momentum fifth waves—classic late-cycle behavior signaling limited upside and elevated reversal risk.
- NVDA support zone is $187–$197; a fifth-wave extension could reach $221–$230 before rolling over.
- Financials and industrials are already in corrective structures (lower lows, three-wave bounces, fading volume), suggesting the broader market top may already be forming.

## Risks / watch-fors
- If NVDA holds above $197 and volume/momentum re-accelerate, the bull-trap thesis is invalidated and the uptrend extends further.
- ASIC share gains (custom silicon from hyperscalers) could structurally compress Nvidia's ~70% gross margins over the medium term.
- Rubin delay timing risk: arriving just as competitive alternatives are scaling is a compounding negative for Nvidia's pricing power.
- Divergences could resolve to the upside if lagging sectors (XLF, XLI, Mag 7) catch up rather than semis rolling over.
