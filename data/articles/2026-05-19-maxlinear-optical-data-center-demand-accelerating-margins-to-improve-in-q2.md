## Thesis
MaxLinear is an under-the-radar optical networking beneficiary whose Keystone DSP platform is ramping at multiple hyperscalers for 400G/800G, with 1.6T product Rushmore set to extend the growth runway into 2027. Management's >40% upward revision to its 2026 optical revenue forecast signals demand is outpacing initial expectations, and the company is on track for its first positive GAAP operating margin in over three years by Q2.

## Key numbers
- 2026 optical data center revenue forecast: raised from $100–130M to $150–170M (>40% increase, midpoint ~$160M)
- Implied 2025 optical revenue: ~$60–70M, implying up to ~180% YoY growth in 2026
- Q1 Infrastructure revenue: $62.8M, +136% YoY, +35% QoQ
- Q2 total revenue guide: $160–170M, ~+52% YoY, ~+20% QoQ
- Q2 GAAP operating margin guide: ~+0.5% (first positive in 3+ years); adjusted ~21%
- Q1 GAAP operating margin: -12.5%; adjusted: +15.9%
- Storage accelerator (Panther) revenue expected to at least double in 2026
- Consensus FY26 revenue: $657M (+40.5% YoY); bull scenario: ~$707M
- Cash: $62.5M; debt: $123.8M; Q1 FCF: -$11.1M (wafer prepayments)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Current 2026 optical growth is entirely 400G/800G-driven; 1.6T (Rushmore) hasn't ramped yet, extending the revenue runway materially into 2027.
- Attach rates for optics roughly double with Nvidia's Vera Rubin platform (1:4–1:6 vs. 1:2–1:3 for Blackwell), amplifying unit volume and ASP tailwinds simultaneously.
- Consensus models sharp deceleration in 2H 2026 (~$8M QoQ) that appears inconsistent with management's "step function" commentary and multi-customer ramp visibility.
- Panther V storage SoC and fiber PON/WiFi 7 broadband ramps provide secondary growth vectors beyond optical DSPs.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Competitive pressure from Broadcom (Taurus, 400G/lane) and Marvell in 1.6T DSPs; MaxLinear is not first to market and must gain share against entrenched incumbents.
- Thin balance sheet ($62.5M cash vs. $123.8M debt) and negative FCF in Q1 due to wafer prepayments; execution risk if demand softens before cash flows recover.
- Hyperscaler customer concentration remains high through 2026; management admits they are "only halfway" to full hyperscale diversification.
- CPO transition timing uncertain; management sees it as 3+ years from being revenue-material, creating potential displacement risk longer term.
