## Thesis
AMD posted a clear inflection quarter driven by surging EPYC server CPU demand tied to agentic AI workloads, while simultaneously doubling its long-term server CPU TAM outlook. The upcoming Helios rack-scale platform and MI450 GPU ramp—anchored by multi-gigawatt partnerships with Meta and OpenAI—position AMD for a step-change in Data Center AI revenue by 2027.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $10.3B (+38% Y/Y), beat high end of guidance
- Q1 EPS (non-GAAP): $1.37 (+43% Y/Y)
- Free cash flow: $2.6B (record; ~3x Y/Y; 25% of revenue)
- Data Center revenue: $5.8B (+57% Y/Y, +7% Q/Q); operating margin 28% (vs. 25% prior year)
- Server CPU revenue: +50%+ Y/Y for fourth consecutive record quarter
- Q2 guidance: ~$11.2B revenue (+46% Y/Y, +9% Q/Q at midpoint)
- Q2 server CPU growth guided: >70% Y/Y
- Server CPU TAM revised: $60B at 18% CAGR → $120B at 35% CAGR by 2030
- Non-GAAP gross margin: 55.0% (+170 bps Y/Y); Q2 guided ~56%
- Cash/short-term investments: $12.3B; debt: $3.2B

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Agentic AI is driving incremental CPU demand (orchestration, data movement, parallel execution), expanding AMD's opportunity independent of GPU competition.
- Management reiterated a target of >50% server CPU market share; Turin (5th-gen EPYC) already crossed 50% of server CPU revenue mix.
- MI450/Helios initial volumes expected Q3, significant ramp Q4 into 2027; Meta partnership covers up to 6 GW, OpenAI adds another 6 GW—12 GW total committed visibility.
- Management expressed confidence in "tens of billions" of annual Data Center AI revenue in 2027 and >80% AI revenue CAGR long-term.
- Venice (6th-gen EPYC, 2nm) ships 2027, claiming >2x throughput per socket vs. leading ARM solutions.

## Risks / watch-fors
- MI450 ramp in Q3–Q4 will initially dilute gross margins below the corporate average; watch for margin compression vs. the 55–58% long-term target.
- Data Center AI revenue dipped sequentially in Q1 due to reduced China exposure; China revenue remains a structural overhang.
- Operating expenses rose 42% Y/Y to $3.1B; sustained heavy R&D and go-to-market spend must be justified by revenue conversion.
