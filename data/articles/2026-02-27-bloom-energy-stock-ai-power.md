## Thesis
The true bottleneck for AI scaling is not compute or software but immediate power availability. Bloom Energy's behind-the-meter solid oxide fuel cells uniquely solve the "time-to-power" problem that grid interconnection queues and sold-out gas turbines cannot. I/O Fund entered at ~$17 in April 2025 and views 2026 as a repeat opportunity given accelerating revenue, expanding backlog, and deepening AI demand.

## Key numbers
- BE stock return 2025: ~291%; I/O Fund avg entry return: ~305% (best entry: 422%)
- FY25 revenue: $2.02B, +37.3% YoY; Q4 beat estimates by ~20.5%
- FY26 guidance: $3.1–$3.3B midpoint (+58% YoY), ~24% above prior consensus
- Total backlog: $20B ($6B product, up 2.5×; $14B service, up 1.5×)
- FY26 adj. EPS guidance: $1.33–$1.48, +~85% YoY, ~25% consensus beat
- FY26 adj. gross margin guided at 32% (+1.7 pts); adj. operating margin 14.1% (+3.2 pts)
- AEP subsidiary deal: $2.65B for substantial portion of 1GW option
- Quanta/QMN order: ~$502M for three microgrid systems
- Brookfield partnership: up to $5B investment in Bloom fuel cell tech
- Forward P/S: 12.7× vs. historical avg ~4.6×; forward P/E: ~104×
- Blackwell power escalation: 70kW (H200) → 120–140kW (GB200/300) → 300–600kW projected next-gen (5× increase)
- BCG: gen AI power demand CAGR 65% through 2028; inference at 122% CAGR
- Wells Fargo: AI power demand +8,050% from 2024 to 2030 (8 TWh → 652 TWh)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Grid interconnection queues (ERCOT ~226GW queued vs. 23GW added) structurally favor behind-the-meter solutions for years.
- GE Vernova gas turbines sold out through 2028; SMRs not viable at scale until 2030s—Bloom has limited near-term competition for rapid deployment.
- Capacity doubling to 2GW in 2026 provides supply to capture near-term demand; $20B backlog equals ~6× FY25 revenue.
- Oracle 55-day deployment and Brookfield/Nvidia partnership validate commercial traction at hyperscaler scale.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Valuation is stretched: forward P/S of 12.7× is nearly 3× the historical average; requires continued execution to justify.
- GAAP profitability remains thin (FY25 GAAP operating margin 3.6%; GAAP EPS still negative for full year).
- Full-year free cash flow margin is low (~2.8%); heavy reliance on Q4 seasonality for cash generation.
- Convertible note raise ($2.5B) increased gross debt to $2.61B, adding financial leverage risk if growth disappoints.
- Natural gas dependency: fuel flexibility is improving but regulatory or supply shifts could pressure economics.
