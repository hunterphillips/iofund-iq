## Thesis
Big Tech is collectively spending ~$530B on AI infrastructure in 2026, but the more important question is who is converting that spend into measurable revenue. I/O Fund's view is that AI hardware/infrastructure suppliers still outperform Big Tech broadly, but a monetization wave driven by inference is approaching, making it worth tracking which Big Tech name is best positioned.

## Key numbers
- Big Tech combined AI capex 2026: ~$530B
- QQQ 2025 return: 20.2% vs. SMH: 48.7%
- **MSFT**: Azure Q3 growth 40% YoY; AI contributing ~22pts to growth; estimated AI revenue ~$25-26B annualized; capex +75% YoY to $34.9B; operating margin 48.9%; net cash $58.8B
- **GOOGL**: Cloud revenue +34% YoY to $15.2B; cloud backlog +46% QoQ to $155B; estimated AI revenue ~$8-10B annualized (GCP + Search); capex +83% YoY to $23.95B; 2025 capex guidance $91-93B; net cash $76.9B
- **AMZN**: AWS +20.2% YoY to $33B (fastest since Q4 2022); estimated AI revenue $10-12B annualized; capex +55% YoY to $35.1B; 2025 capex guidance $125B; operating margin 11.3%
- **META**: Advantage+ at $60B annual run rate; ad revenue +25.6% YoY; capex +111% YoY to $19.4B; Reality Labs cumulative losses $73B; net cash $15.7B
- **AAPL**: Revenue +7.9% YoY; services revenue $28.8B (+15.1%); operating margin 31.7%

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Google is ranked the strongest Big Tech AI play: dual monetization via Search advertising (immediate) and Cloud (accelerating), plus custom Ironwood TPU silicon improving unit economics.
- Meta's Advantage+ is the second-largest AI revenue segment in the market (~$60B run rate), yet this is widely underreported; near-term monetization is platform-native, not LLM commercialization.
- Microsoft's AI revenue is still less than 1/5 of a single year's capex, making the ROI case difficult for the market to accept near-term.
- Big Tech broadly is not expected to lead the AI trade in H1 2026; hardware/infrastructure suppliers remain the preferred allocation.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Big Tech absorbing grid/electricity costs internally (per Microsoft's Brad Smith) inflates effective capex cost per GW and pressures margins industry-wide.
- Meta's off-balance-sheet data center financing via Blue Owl SPV obscures true leverage; Llama adoption by developers remains weak vs. OpenAI/Anthropic.
- AWS faces risk of losing IaaS leadership in the AI era if custom Trainium chips and startup programs don't close the gap with Azure and GCP.
