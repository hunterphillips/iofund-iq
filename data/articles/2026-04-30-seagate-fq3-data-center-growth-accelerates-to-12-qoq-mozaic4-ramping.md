## Thesis
Seagate's FQ3 demonstrated a meaningful inflection in data center growth, with pricing and product mix improving as Mozaic4+ begins its ramp. Supply is largely allocated through CY27, demand is outpacing capacity, and EPS is projected to grow at more than 2x the rate of revenue through FY27 as HAMR margin leverage flows disproportionately to the bottom line.

## Key numbers
- Data Center revenue: $2.5B, +55% YoY / +12% QoQ (accelerated from +28% YoY in FQ2)
- Total revenue: $3.11B, +44% YoY, beat estimates by ~5%
- FQ4 guidance: $3.45B midpoint, +41% YoY, beat estimates by ~9.5%
- Adjusted EPS FQ3: $4.10, +116% YoY, beat by ~17%; FQ4 guided at $5.00 ±$0.20 (+93% YoY)
- Adjusted gross margin: record; GAAP gross margin 46.5%, +11.3 pts YoY
- Incremental gross margin: 70% (above 50% Analyst Day target)
- Adjusted operating margin: 37.5%, guided to low-40s in FQ4
- Free cash flow: $953M (30.6% margin), >4x YoY; TTM FCF $1.99B vs. $818M all of FY25
- Net leverage: 0.7x (down from 2.5x five quarters ago)
- Nearline exabytes shipped: 175 EB, +47% YoY / +6% QoQ
- Revenue per TB: mid-single digit increase YoY and QoQ
- Morgan Stanley channel checks: hyperscalers negotiating ~$20/TB for CY27-28 vs. current ~$13-15/TB

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Mozaic4+ (up to 44TB/drive) began revenue shipments in March; expected to surpass Mozaic3 share and represent the majority of HAMR exabyte shipments by end of CY26, reaching ~70% of nearline exabytes by end of CY27.
- Supply is essentially sold out through CY27; planning discussions already underway for CY28, with build-to-order contracts being finalized for FY27.
- Seagate raised its long-term annual growth outlook from low-to-mid teens to >20%, supported by inference, agentic AI, and physical AI demand (e.g., a single AV generating ~4TB/hour).
- Mozaic4+ carries a similar bill-of-materials to Mozaic3 but higher ASP, making the ramp a dual revenue and margin catalyst.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Unit volumes are flat and not expected to grow materially without an edge/IoT resurgence, making pricing and mix the primary growth levers—any pricing softness would disproportionately hurt revenue.
- HDD pricing lags NAND/SSD despite tight supply; if hyperscaler negotiations don't close near the ~$20/TB level suggested by MS channel checks, the bull case on CY27 revenue could disappoint.
- Mozaic4+ ramp concentration at two largest CSPs initially; remaining customer qualifications expected in FQ4—delays would push out revenue contribution.
