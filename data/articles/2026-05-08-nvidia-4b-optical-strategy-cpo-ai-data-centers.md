## Thesis
Co-packaged optics (CPO) represents a structural, not cyclical, shift in AI networking as Nvidia's pod-scaling roadmap physically exhausts copper's reach. Nvidia is cementing its CPO supply chain with $2B equity stakes each in Coherent and Lumentum, and a $2B investment in Marvell. Companies qualifying into Nvidia's and Broadcom's CPO supply chains stand to be among the largest beneficiaries.

## Key numbers
- Nvidia networking revenue: ~$11B; Broadcom AI networking: ~$3.6B (one-third of $10.7B AI total)
- NVL576 pod width: ~5 meters — exceeds AEC copper reach of 3 meters at 200G per lane
- CPO vs. pluggable transceivers: up to 5x better power efficiency; signal loss ~4 dB vs. ~22 dB
- Marvell CPO revenue target: $500M annualized run rate by Q4 FY2028, doubling to $1B by Q4 FY2029
- Credo optical revenue target: $500M in FY2027 following DustPhotonics acquisition
- TrendForce: CPO penetration rises from <1% in 2026 to >35% by 2030; total optical shipments grow from ~50M to ~200M units
- McKinsey: inference data center demand reaches 93 GW by 2030 (35% CAGR) vs. training at 62 GW (22% CAGR)
- Lumentum: revenue +65% YoY to $665.5M last quarter; guiding ~90% YoY growth next quarter; AI TAM seen growing 5x to $90B+ by 2030
- Coherent: data center revenue +34% YoY; book-to-bill >4x; CPO SAM >$15B by 2030
- Corning: Enterprise segment +61% YoY to $3B in 2025; scale-up CPO opportunity estimated 2–3x that, implying $6–9B incremental
- Valuation: all three trade at forward P/E 2.2–2.6x their 3-year medians (COHR 54x vs. 25x median; LITE 74x vs. 33x; GLW 52x vs. 20x)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Nvidia's roadmap makes CPO mandatory at NVL576 (Rubin Ultra) and universal at NVL1152 (Feynman); this is a hard architectural forcing function, not a preference.
- Broadcom's Meta study (15M device-hours, no unserviceable failures, 5x serviceable failure improvement) is the first meaningful real-world reliability data — a key adoption gating factor.
- Fiber density explodes with CPO ("tens of thousands of fibers per rack"), making Corning a less-obvious but significant beneficiary.
- Supply chain qualification within Nvidia/Broadcom CPO programs is the key alpha source; lesser-known names may outperform headline stocks.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Valuations are stretched — all three optical names near all-time-high forward P/E multiples after 210–950% runs since mid-2025; much of the CPO upside may already be priced in.
- CPO reliability in live data centers (not labs) remains unproven; slow real-world validation in 2026–2027 could delay the adoption inflection.
- Both Jensen Huang and Hock Tan explicitly favor extending copper as long as possible, suggesting CPO ramp timing is uncertain and bridge technologies (NPO, LPO) could delay share gains.
