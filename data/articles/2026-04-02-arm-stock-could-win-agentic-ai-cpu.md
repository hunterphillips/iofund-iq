## Thesis
Agentic AI is shifting the compute bottleneck from GPUs to CPUs, as orchestration tasks (tool calls, API requests, memory management) account for 50–90% of total workflow latency. Arm's power-efficient architecture and new in-house AGI CPU position it to capture a meaningful share of a projected $100B data center CPU TAM, while its recurring licensing/royalty model provides a durable earnings floor.

## Key numbers
- CPU latency share of agentic workflows: 50–90%
- Expected CPU core density increase: 4X (30M → 120M cores/GW)
- Token generation increase per user in multi-agent systems: up to 15X
- AGI CPU performance vs. x86: ~2X per rack at lower power (300W vs. 390–500W for AMD EPYC)
- Air-cooled rack density: 8,160 cores in 36kW; liquid-cooled: 45,696 cores in ~100kW effective draw
- Claimed capex savings: up to $10B per GW of AI data center capacity
- AGI CPU revenue target: >$1B over FY2027–28, scaling to $15B by FY2031
- Total FY2031 revenue guidance: $25B (consensus: $21.2B, ~18% below management)
- Royalty revenue CAGR target: ~20% over next 5 years
- ACV growth: 28% YoY for three consecutive quarters, reaching $1.62B
- Gross margin: ~97.6%; adjusted operating margin: 40.7%
- Forward P/S: 28.6x (vs. Broadcom 14.4x, Nvidia 11.6x)
- Cash/short-term investments: $3.54B; no debt

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Arm's AGI CPU directly competes with x86 incumbents and even Nvidia's Vera CPU, offering higher core counts and 2.5X more memory capacity per rack than Vera.
- Meta is the launch customer, having co-developed the chip over ~2.5 years, providing credible early validation.
- Merchant CPU revenue is a new, high-margin growth vector layered on top of already-sticky licensing/royalty streams; management raised long-term adjusted operating margin guidance by 500 bps.
- Consensus estimates lag management guidance by ~18% on revenue and ~7% on EPS for FY2031—potential upside if AGI CPU ramp executes.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Valuation is stretched at ~28–36x P/S versus semiconductor peers; merchant CPU revenue will take years to scale, leaving the stock exposed if ramp disappoints.
- Arm must compete against hyperscaler custom Arm-based CPUs (Graviton, Axion, Cobalt) that its own licensees built—cannibalizing potential customers.
- Nvidia's standalone Vera CPU racks target the same agentic orchestration market, with deep ecosystem integration advantages.
- Licensing revenue is lumpy quarter-to-quarter; ACV is a better trend indicator but can mask near-term volatility.
- High stock-based compensation (~23% of revenue) creates a wide gap between GAAP and adjusted margins.
