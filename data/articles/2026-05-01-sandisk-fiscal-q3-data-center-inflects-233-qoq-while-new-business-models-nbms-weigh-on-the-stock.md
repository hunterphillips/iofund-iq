## Thesis
SanDisk delivered one of the strongest quarters in NAND history, with revenue nearly doubling sequentially and data center demand inflecting sharply. Management's CY26 data center growth forecast has risen roughly 4x over nine months, and the demand-supply gap is widening each quarter. New Business Models (NBMs)—multi-year volume commitments with variable pricing—are being misread by the market as margin-capping, but they actually combine demand certainty with pricing upside.

## Key numbers
- FQ3 revenue: $5.95B (+251% YoY, +97% QoQ); beat estimates by ~26%
- Data center revenue: $1.47B (+645% YoY, +233% QoQ); now 25% of revenue vs. 15% prior quarter
- Edge revenue: $3.66B (+295% YoY, +118% QoQ)
- Gross margin: 78.4% (+27.5 pts QoQ, +55.9 pts YoY)
- Adjusted EPS: $23.41, beat estimates by ~60%; GAAP EPS $23.03
- FQ4 guidance: $7.75–$8.25B revenue (+321% YoY, +34.5% QoQ); adj. EPS $30–$33 vs. $23.44 estimate
- NBM remaining performance obligations (RPO): $42B; financial guarantees exceed $11B across 5 agreements
- NBMs cover >1/3 of BiCS supply in FY27; management targets >50%
- Cash: $3.74B; zero debt; $6B buyback authorized
- Operating cash flow: $3.04B (51% of revenue)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Variable pricing in NBMs means SanDisk retains upside if spot prices rise; market concern about margin caps appears overstated.
- QLC "Stargate" SKUs (128TB, then 256TB) entering volume shipments in FQ4, layering incremental revenue on top of already-strong TLC demand driven by KV cache inference workloads.
- Management guided conservatively for FQ4 due to genuine pricing uncertainty, not sandbagging tactics—actual results could again exceed guidance materially.
- CY26 data center growth forecast revised from ~20s → 40s → 60s → mid-70s over three consecutive quarters, signaling persistent structural demand.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Pricing deceleration: FQ4 EPS guide implies some pricing normalization after an unusually fast FQ3 acceleration; trajectory beyond FQ4 is uncertain.
- Growth stock peak risk: analyst notes that demand signal weakening—not multi-year contracts—is the typical leading indicator of a top; monitor hyperscaler capex and NAND bit demand closely.
- NBM execution: five deals signed but negotiations ongoing; failure to reach the >50% supply coverage target could reduce earnings predictability.
- Inventory build: inventory rose 13.7% QoQ to $2.24B, partly to support Stargate ramp—watch for any demand shortfall that could pressure this buffer.
