## Thesis
Arm's AGI CPU demand visibility doubled to $2B across FY27–28 in just six weeks, but near-term revenue is supply-capped at $1B, pushing meaningful contribution to FY28. The stock's post-earnings reversal (from +7% to -6.4%) reflects investor frustration that the headline demand signal won't translate to revenue for 1–2 years. Long-term, Arm's IP royalty engine and data center share gains remain intact, but valuation must contend with a slow AGI CPU ramp.

## Key numbers
- Q4 FY26 revenue: $1.49B (+20% YoY, +20% QoQ), beating guidance midpoint by ~1.4%
- FY26 full-year revenue: $4.92B (+23% YoY); third consecutive year of 20%+ growth
- AGI CPU demand visibility: $2B across FY27–28 (doubled from $1B six weeks prior)
- AGI CPU revenue outlook maintained: $1B; first shipment revenue ~$90–100M in Q4 FY27 (March 2027), ~$910M in FY28
- AGI CPU as % of FY28 consensus ($7.61B): ~12%
- Potential upside if $2B demand converts at 80%: ~$800M above consensus
- Data center royalties: more than doubled YoY; expected to double again in FY27
- Royalty revenue Q4: $671M (+11% YoY, -9% QoQ); license revenue: $819M (+29% YoY, +62% QoQ)
- ACV growth: +22% YoY (decelerated 6 pts from prior three quarters)
- Adjusted EPS Q4: $0.60 (+9% YoY); FY26 full-year: $1.77 (+8.6%)
- Non-GAAP operating margin Q4: 49.1% (down from 52.8% YoY); opex grew 30% YoY
- FY26 operating cash flow: $1.52B (31% margin)
- Cash/short-term investments: $3.60B; no debt

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Supply chain—not demand—is the binding constraint on AGI CPU revenue; Arm must secure wafers, memory, packaging, and test equipment before raising its $1B target.
- Primary AGI CPU customers are enterprises (e.g., Cloudflare, SAP, SK Telecom), not hyperscalers, who already design their own Arm-based chips (Graviton, Axion). This limits near-term TAM capture.
- AMD's server CPU TAM forecast jumped to $120B by 2030 (+20% vs. Arm's own $100B estimate); AMD targets >50% share (~$60B) vs. Arm's $15B FY31 target (~12% share)—implying intense competition.
- Arm's CEO believes Arm will hold the largest CPU architecture market share by end of decade, driven by near-100% attach rate to AI accelerators over time.
- Opex growth expected to decelerate from 26% CAGR (FY24–26) to mid-teens through FY31, setting up future operating leverage.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Supply chain resolution timeline: delays in wafer/memory/packaging procurement could push AGI CPU revenue further into FY28 or beyond.
- Hyperscaler bypass risk: large cloud providers designing custom Arm CPUs in-house may never be meaningful AGI CPU customers, capping the addressable merchant market.
- Competitive pressure: AMD targeting 4x Arm's projected AI server CPU revenue by 2030; Intel remains a factor.
- RPO declined 7% YoY for third consecutive quarter; management attributes this to faster revenue conversion, but warrants monitoring.
- TSMC recently exited its Arm equity position—a subtle negative signal worth watching.
