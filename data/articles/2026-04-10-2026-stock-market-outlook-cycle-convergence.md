## Thesis
A multi-quarter topping process is underway across U.S. and global markets, confirmed by synchronized technical breakdowns and the rare simultaneous convergence of Gann's 60-year Great Cycle with the 4-year Presidential Cycle. The current bounce is expected to be a bear-market trap rather than a resumption of the bull trend, though a weekly QQQ close at all-time highs would invalidate the defensive stance.

## Key numbers
- S&P 500 prior resistance level: 6,780 (bull/bear line in the sand)
- S&P 500 March 30 low: ~6,316 (down ~400+ pts from breakdown)
- Mag 7 weighting in S&P 500: ~30%
- Presidential Cycle Year 2 positive-return frequency: ~53%; produces the cycle low 56% of the time (9 of 16 cycles since 1950)
- QQQ Wave 4 target range: $500–$445

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Every Mag 7 stock topped between July 2025 and February 2026, diverging from the index's final high on Feb 25—a classic late-bull warning.
- Completed 5-wave advances followed by first lower lows are visible across Financials (XLF), Small Caps (IWM), Industrials (XLI), German DAX, and Canadian TSX—a globally synchronized topping signal.
- The composite cycle roadmap calls for a bounce (late April–early May) that restores false confidence before the next volatility leg resumes.
- Bullish pivot trigger: QQQ closes a full week at a new all-time high—that flips the primary scenario to Scenario 3 (one more swing to new highs into Fall 2026).

## Risks / watch-fors
- Scenario 2 (red): All waves needed to complete the entire bull market off the 2022 low are already in place; an ending diagonal pattern could mean the correction is a full cycle top, not just a Wave 4.
- Cycles can break their historical rhythm; the 60-year and Presidential Cycle correlation is probabilistic, not deterministic—monitor for divergence from the composite roadmap.
- Current bounce may be deeper/longer than expected, drawing in buyers before the next leg down accelerates.
