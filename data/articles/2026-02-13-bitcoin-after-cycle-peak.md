## Thesis
Bitcoin has likely completed a 5-wave Elliott Wave advance from the 2022 low and entered a new bear cycle. Narrative-driven investing repeatedly misleads crypto participants; technical/sentiment analysis has been more reliable for timing entries and exits. A meaningful bounce is probable before a deeper final leg lower completes the cycle.

## Key numbers
- Prior buy alerts issued: 13, ranging from ~$25K to ~$60K
- Sell alerts issued: 9, reducing exposure between $95K and $113K
- Bitcoin decline from last free-article high: ~48%
- Expected bear-market bounce target: $84K–$107K
- Possible final minor low before bounce: ~$50K region
- Upside breakout level that would force re-evaluation: above $107K
- Projected ultimate cycle trough if bear thesis holds: $30K–$40K
- Key short-term confirmation level: reclaim of $72.5K signals low already in

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Treat any rally toward $84K–$107K as a bear-market rally unless Bitcoin breaks above $107K impulsively; choppy/overlapping structure would confirm the bear case.
- A dip into the $50Ks, if it occurs, should arrive on lighter volume/momentum than the prior leg—use that as a signal it is the final minor wave, not a new collapse.
- Coinbase (COIN) is being monitored as a corroborating indicator given its high correlation to BTC price action.
- The firm has already reduced most BTC exposure; re-entry is contingent on cycle-low confirmation, not narrative.

## Risks / watch-fors
- A clean break above $107K on strong, impulsive volume would invalidate the bear thesis and require reassessing probability of new all-time highs.
- Macro/narrative traps: new bullish stories will likely emerge during the bounce phase, tempting premature re-entry before the $30K–$40K ultimate low is reached.
- RSI and volume internals remain key monitors—failure to reclaim bull-market RSI support zone (~33.5) on any bounce would reinforce the bearish structure.
