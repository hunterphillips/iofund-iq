## Thesis
Broadcom delivered record revenue with AI semiconductor growth accelerating for a fourth consecutive quarter, and its customer base is diversifying beyond Google to six hyperscalers. However, a communication failure around rack-vs-chip economics caused a Q3 AI revenue guide miss, and a new financing arrangement that lends Broadcom's credit rating to fund unprofitable AI customers introduces a structural risk the analyst views as circular investing.

## Key numbers
- Q2 revenue: $22.2B, +48% YoY; beat consensus by ~0.3%
- Q2 AI semiconductor revenue: $10.8B, +143% YoY, +29% QoQ
- Q3 revenue guide: $29.4B (~84% YoY), above consensus of $28.5B
- Q3 AI revenue guide: $16.0B (~200% YoY); missed $17.2B estimate
- FY26 AI revenue guide: $56B (+180% YoY); FY27 target reiterated at >$100B
- AI bookings vs. shipments: $30B booked vs. $10.8B shipped (~3x coverage)
- Networking share of AI revenue: ~40% this quarter, expected to shift to ~30% as XPUs grow
- Circular financing vehicle: $35–36B private credit facility with Apollo/Blackstone; Broadcom backstops ~$25B at ~5.75% vs. unbacked tranches at 8–9%
- FCF: $10.26B, 46.2% margin, +60% YoY
- Adjusted EPS: $2.44, +54% YoY; beat by 1.7%
- FY27 consensus EPS revised up 27% to $18.50 since March

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- The AI guide miss traces entirely to a shift from rack-level to chip-only supply for Anthropic—lower revenue but better margins; the underlying demand signal (3x bookings-to-shipments, visibility to 2028) remains intact.
- Content per gigawatt is expected to rise as XPUs incorporate more SRAM, embedded CPUs, and multi-die HBM configurations, supporting long-term revenue per unit growth.
- Q4 FY26 implies sequential AI revenue deceleration (30% QoQ vs. 48% in Q3), which management attributes to conservative guidance rather than demand softness.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Circular investing: Broadcom is effectively lending its investment-grade credit rating to manufacture demand from customers (Anthropic, OpenAI) that are not yet profitable, raising questions about long-term credit exposure.
- Supply constraints on non-chip components (HBM, CoWoS, power infrastructure) could delay customer deployments and push out revenue recognition despite strong bookings.
- Management's repeated evasiveness on rack-vs-chip economics across multiple earnings calls before finally clarifying is flagged as a credibility and communication concern.
