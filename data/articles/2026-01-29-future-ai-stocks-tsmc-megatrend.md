## Thesis
TSMC's conservative management explicitly labeled AI a multi-year megatrend, raising long-term growth forecasts and committing record capex—signals that carry unusual weight given the foundry's multi-year planning horizon. Hyperscalers are now approaching TSMC directly to secure wafer capacity, reinforcing that AI infrastructure demand is structural rather than cyclical. As advanced nodes (N2, A16) ramp, TSMC's pricing power and customer dependency deepen.

## Key numbers
- Q4 2025 revenue: $33.73B, +25.5% YoY, beat guidance midpoint by 2.8%
- FY2025 revenue: $122.4B, +35.9% YoY
- Q1 2026 guidance: $34.6–$35.8B, implying ~38% YoY growth
- 2026 revenue growth guidance: ~30% YoY
- AI accelerator CAGR forecast (2024–2029): raised to mid-to-high 50% (from mid-40% prior year)
- Long-term revenue CAGR: raised to 25% (from 20% prior year)
- 2026 capex guidance: $52–$56B (+32% YoY); 70–80% allocated to 7nm and below
- Q4 GAAP EPS: $3.14, +40.2% YoY, beat estimates by 5.2%
- Q4 free cash flow: $11.9B, +48.8% YoY; FCF margin 35.2%
- Advanced nodes (<7nm): 77% of wafer revenue in Q4; 3nm rose to 28%
- AI accelerator share of revenue: high-teens % in 2025, up from mid-teens in 2024

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Raised forecasts and direct hyperscaler outreach signal durable, multi-year demand—not a near-term inventory cycle.
- HPC revenue decelerated in Q3 due to Blackwell→Rubin platform transition; reacceleration expected in 2H 2026 as Rubin (3nm N3P) enters mass production.
- Consensus projects 20%+ EPS growth in both 2026 and 2027, suggesting the earnings expansion is sustained, not a one-year spike.
- TSMC's 3nm pricing premium (~25% over 5nm) and Samsung's yield/efficiency lag (10–20%) reinforce pricing power durability.

## Risks / watch-fors
- CEO C.C. Wei acknowledged nervousness about committing $52–$56B capex annually—if AI demand disappoints, overcapacity risk is significant.
- Geopolitical tensions, tariffs, and overseas fab expansion (Arizona) could pressure margins or complicate supply chains.
- Platform transitions (e.g., Blackwell to Rubin) create temporary HPC revenue air pockets; watch for reacceleration confirmation in 2H 2026.
