## Thesis
Dell delivered a historic beat-and-raise quarter, with agentic AI driving unexpected demand for traditional CPU-based servers, storage, and services—a TAM expansion Dell's own management admitted they did not foresee as recently as October 2025. The shift from AI as "adviser" to AI as "operator" is creating structural, not cyclical, demand across Dell's entire infrastructure stack.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $43.8B, +87.5% YoY, +31.3% QoQ (beat consensus by ~$8.5B)
- Q2 guidance midpoint: $44.5B, +49.4% YoY (beat consensus by ~$7.9B)
- FY27 revenue guide raised to $165–169B (from $138–142B), implying +47% YoY
- ISG revenue: $29.0B, +181% YoY; AI server revenue: $16.1B, +757% YoY, +80% QoQ
- AI server backlog: $51.3B (up from $43B in Q4); orders: $24.4B
- FY27 AI server guide raised to $60B (+144% YoY, up from $50B prior)
- Traditional Servers & Networking: $8.5B, +92% YoY
- Adjusted EPS: $4.86, +214% YoY (beat consensus of $2.90 by ~68%)
- FY27 adjusted EPS guide: $17.65–18.15 midpoint, +74% YoY
- DoD contract win: $9.7B over five years; IREN deal: $1.6B

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Agentic AI's orchestration layer runs on CPUs, not GPUs—each GPU call requires significant CPU resources, directly expanding Dell's traditional server TAM in ways management couldn't model months ago.
- Storage and services attach rates are rising alongside server demand, as agents require high-performance storage for audit trails and state management.
- Supply, not demand, is the binding constraint; CPU lead times extend ~one year out, pushing customers into multi-year supply agreements and locking in orders before pricing is even set.
- The full-year guide absorbed the Q1/Q2 beats rather than treating them as pull-forwards, signaling management confidence in 2H demand.

## Risks / watch-fors
- AI server revenue guided down ~3.7% QoQ in Q2; lumpiness is structural and requires active position management.
- Gross margins compressed to 17.8% GAAP (from 21.1% YoY) due to AI server mix; further margin pressure is possible if that mix grows.
- Some customers are buying ahead to lock in supply, introducing a modest pull-forward risk if component constraints ease suddenly.
- FY27 full-year guide implies less visibility (or conservatism) into 2H, given Q1+Q2 combined beats already total ~$16.4B.
