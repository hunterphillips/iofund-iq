## Thesis
Google Cloud's acceleration to 63% YoY growth—the largest hyperscaler acceleration seen in the AI cycle—combined with the decision to sell TPUs directly to third-party customers marks a structural shift in the custom silicon vs. GPU narrative. Backlog nearly doubling to $462B in a single quarter signals durable multi-year demand, though capex growing faster than revenue remains the key near-term tension.

## Key numbers
- Cloud revenue: $20B, +63% YoY, +13% QoQ (vs. 48% in Q4 2025, 34% in Q3 2025)
- Cloud operating margin: 32.9% (vs. 17.8% a year ago); operating income tripled to $6.6B
- Cloud backlog: $462B, +90% QoQ from $243B, +400% YoY; ~half converts within 24 months
- Total revenue: $109.9B, +21.8% YoY, beat estimates by ~2.7%
- Capex: $35.7B, +107% YoY; full-year 2026 guidance raised to ~$185B midpoint
- Free cash flow: $10.1B, down ~47% YoY due to capex surge
- Search revenue: $60.4B, +19% YoY
- TPU v8 (8t): 2.7x performance/dollar vs. Ironwood, 121 exaFLOPs FP4 per pod, 9,600 chips/pod
- TPU v8 (8i): 1,152 chips/pod, 331.8 TB HBM (7x prior gen)
- Anthropic deal: $40B commitment ($10B now, $30B milestone-gated); 5GW Cloud capacity over 5 years; Anthropic valued at $350B

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Google entering merchant TPU sales to external customers (AI labs, capital markets, HPC) is the most direct competitive challenge to Nvidia's GPU dominance to date.
- The 8i chip's 7x HBM expansion targets memory-bound inference workloads, enabling lower cost-per-token and aggressive API pricing while sustaining margin expansion—a compounding flywheel.
- Backlog surge is partly Anthropic-driven, so the QoQ jump may not fully reflect organic enterprise demand; revenue realization is weighted toward 2027.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Capex (+107% YoY) is outpacing Cloud revenue growth (+63%), compressing FCF significantly; macro deterioration could make this ratio a focal point for investors.
- Backlog concentration risk: Anthropic's large multi-year deal inflates the QoQ backlog figure; underlying GCP organic demand growth warrants separate scrutiny.
- TPU merchant sales are nascent—only a "small percent" of TPU hardware revenue expected in 2026, with the majority in 2027; execution and customer adoption remain unproven at scale.
