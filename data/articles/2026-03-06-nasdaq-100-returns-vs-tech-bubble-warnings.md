## Thesis
Persistent "tech bubble" warnings over the past decade have been repeatedly wrong, costing sidelined investors a 550% Nasdaq-100 gain. Today's AI cycle differs structurally from the dot-com era due to supply constraints, real revenues, and profitable operators — though a meaningful near-term correction remains probable given deteriorating market internals.

## Key numbers
- Nasdaq-100 gain over 10 years from 2015 low: ~550%
- Shopify 10-year return: ~5,200%; Nvidia: ~22,000%
- Nasdaq-100 annual returns during "bubble" years: +8.4% (2015), +5.9% (2016), +31.5% (2017), +53.8% (2023), +24.9% (2024)
- Nvidia forward P/E: ~21.7x — below its pre-May 2023 blowout earnings multiple despite +475% share gain
- Big Tech capex 2026: ~$655B, up ~60% YoY
- TSMC advanced-node capacity: ~3x short of AI demand
- One DRAM type price surge: +75% in one month (Dec–Jan)
- Wells Fargo AI power demand projection: +550% by 2026, +8,050% vs. 2024 baseline by 2030
- OpenAI revenue: $1B (2023) → ~$20B annualized (2025)
- Meta AI ads run rate: ~$60B in 2025 (~3.5 years from launch)
- Global SaaS companies: 30,800+, signaling late-cycle saturation
- NAAIM sentiment: 78th–96th percentile bullish readings since late October 2025

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Supply constraints across compute, memory, networking, and power make broad AI oversupply unlikely near-term, structurally differentiating this cycle from dot-com.
- AI companies are generating substantial profits now; comparing net margins of AppLovin (66%), TSMC (48%), Reddit (35%) to Amazon's dot-com-era -56% illustrates the difference.
- Cloud software is late-cycle (growth collapsed 2022–2023, 30,800+ SaaS competitors); rotating away from cloud into AI infrastructure remains the preferred positioning.
- AI is still transitioning from training to inference — the largest monetization phase has not yet begun.

## Risks / watch-fors
- All 7 Mag-7 stocks making lower highs while S&P 500 makes higher highs — a historically reliable warning signal (~32% of S&P 500 weight).
- Margin debt at record highs (above 2021 peak); NAAIM and AAII sentiment at historically elevated levels consistent with prior market tops.
- Key S&P 500 support at 6,780–6,720; a break targets 6,500–6,300 as the first leg of a larger correction with a lower-high bounce into late 2026.
