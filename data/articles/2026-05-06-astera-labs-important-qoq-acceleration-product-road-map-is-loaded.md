## Thesis
Astera Labs is defying the typical post-IPO growth fade, beating Q1 2026 expectations with 14% QoQ growth (vs. 8% expected) and accelerating YoY growth to 93.4%. The company is expanding well beyond PCIe retimers into a multi-vector AI inference infrastructure play spanning smart fabric switching, CXL memory, optical interconnects, and NVLink Fusion—each representing incremental content-per-accelerator upside.

## Key numbers
- Q1 2026 revenue: $308.4M (+93.4% YoY, +14% QoQ; beat estimates by 5.5%)
- Q2 2026 guidance midpoint: $360M (+87.6% YoY, +16.7% QoQ; beat estimates by 16.1%)
- PCIe Gen 6 revenue: >1/3 of total Q1 revenue
- Scorpio product mix: ~15% of revenue in CY25, targeting ~50% by end of CY26
- Target content per accelerator: >$1,000
- Adjusted gross margin: 76.4% (+150 bps YoY); Q2 guided at 73% (warrant-related ~200 bps headwind)
- Adjusted EPS: $0.61 (+84.8% YoY, beat by 13.5%); Q2 guide $0.69 midpoint (+56.8% YoY)
- Free cash flow: $67M (21.7% margin) vs. $5.97M (3.7%) a year ago
- Cash & marketable securities: $1.18B, zero debt

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Scorpio X-Series 320-lane switch (5.12 TB/s bidirectional, single ASIC) is the largest open memory-semantic fabric switch available, with in-network compute that offloads collective operations—directly addressing MoE inference bottlenecks.
- Second-half 2026 is heavily weighted toward Scorpio X-Series ramp; X-Series expected to surpass P-Series and become the largest product line by year-end, driving ASP expansion.
- NVLink Fusion design win in progress with NVIDIA and a hyperscaler; revenue contribution expected in 2027, broadening Astera's reach regardless of UALink vs. ESUN protocol outcome.
- Near-packaged optics (NPO) ramp targeted for 2027; co-packaged optics mainstream in 2028—optical acquisition extends the TAM into longer-reach GPU cluster connectivity.
- CXL Leo controller ramping with Microsoft Azure M-Series VMs; new KV cache offload design win with 2027 shipments adds memory-side AI infrastructure exposure.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Q2 gross margin guided down ~340 bps sequentially to 73% due to a customer warrant agreement—watch whether this is a one-time drag or signals pricing pressure.
- UALink vs. ESUN protocol competition could delay or limit Scorpio X-Series adoption; market sentiment has already punished the stock ~55% peak-to-trough on such concerns.
- Scorpio X ramp is back-half weighted, creating execution risk if hyperscaler capex cycles shift or supply chain issues emerge.
- NVLink Fusion and CXL revenues are 2027 events; near-term story depends heavily on PCIe Gen 6 and Scorpio P/X momentum sustaining through 2026.
