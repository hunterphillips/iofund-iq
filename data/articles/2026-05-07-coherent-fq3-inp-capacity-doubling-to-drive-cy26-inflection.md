## Thesis
Coherent is at an inflection point driven by a 2x expansion of indium phosphide (InP) capacity by end of CY2026, with a 4x increase targeted over two years. The 6-inch wafer ramp is running a quarter ahead of schedule, yielding better than the legacy 3-inch process, and management expects FQ4 (June quarter) to mark a clear acceleration in revenue growth. Multiple emerging product lines—OCS, CPO/NPO, scale-across, and multi-rail—add further upside beyond the core transceiver business.

## Key numbers
- FQ3 revenue: $1.81B (+21% YoY, +7% QoQ; +27% YoY pro forma excluding divested units)
- Datacenter & Communications segment: $1.36B (+41% YoY, +13% QoQ)
- Communications sub-segment: +60% YoY, +16% QoQ
- Adjusted gross margin: 39.6% (+110 bps YoY); guided to 40% in FQ4
- Adjusted EPS: $1.41 (+55% YoY); FQ4 guide midpoint implies +62% YoY
- FQ4 revenue guidance: $1.91B–$2.05B (~+30% YoY, ~+10% QoQ at midpoint)
- Capex: $290M in FQ3 (+159% YoY); expected to rise sequentially in FQ4
- Free cash flow: -$384M in FQ3 vs. +$51M a year ago
- OCS addressable market: ~$4B (recently doubled estimate per management)
- Debt leverage: 0.5x (down from 1.7x last quarter), aided by $2B Nvidia investment

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- The lag between InP device production and transceiver shipment is ~2–3 months, explaining why capacity gains haven't yet fully translated to revenue; management confirmed the inflection should appear in the June quarter.
- Scale-across (DCI) is currently the fastest-growing segment driver and is supported by long-term customer agreements; multi-rail systems are expected to begin shipping in 1H CY2027.
- CPO/NPO first revenue is expected in 2H CY2026, with Coherent supplying high-content solutions (lasers, fiber attach units, isolators, TECs) rather than just components.
- FY2027 (starting July 2026) growth rate is guided to exceed FY2026's rate, suggesting sustained acceleration.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Heavy cash burn and rising capex create near-term balance sheet pressure; free cash flow was deeply negative this quarter.
- Revenue recognition lag means the capacity-to-revenue thesis requires patience—execution must be verified each quarter.
- Industrial segment remains a drag (-16% YoY reported), and broader industrial softness could persist.
- Customer qualification timelines for 6-inch devices could slip, delaying the expected inflection.
