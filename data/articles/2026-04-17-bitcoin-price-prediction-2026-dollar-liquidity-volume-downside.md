## Thesis
The analyst argues Bitcoin entered a new bear market cycle after peaking near $126K in October 2025, driven by a completed dollar bear market and deteriorating volume patterns. The DXY inverse correlation and distribution-phase volume behavior both point to further downside before a durable low forms. Long-term structural bull case remains intact due to sovereign debt dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Bitcoin peak: ~$126K (Oct 6, 2025), ~19 days after DXY completed its bear market pattern (Sep 17, 2025)
- Bitcoin decline since peak: >52%
- Key support: $62,534 — break below targets $55K–$40K range; highest-probability zone $48K–$46K
- Bull scenario invalidation: sustained rally above $79,125; expected to fail below $106K–$116K resistance
- DXY confirmation level: break above $100.60 would confirm new dollar uptrend
- ~64% of global debt denominated in USD; ~80% of global lending requires collateral
- ~75% of global financial transactions involve debt refinancing, not new issuance
- I/O Fund sold >80% of crypto exposure between Nov 2024–Oct 2025
- U.S. Debt/GDP: 121%; global total: 236%; debt service = 31% of U.S. tax receipts
- Projected U.S. deficit: 5.8% of GDP this year, averaging 6.1% over next decade

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Current bounce is on decelerating volume with RSI in bear-rally exhaustion range — consistent with another leg lower.
- Volume pattern flipped in March 2025: expanding on declines, contracting on bounces — classic distribution signal mirroring 2022 structure.
- A confirmed DXY uptrend would extend liquidity tightening headwinds for Bitcoin and risk assets broadly.
- Team remains defensively positioned; waiting for targets to be reached before re-entering meaningfully.

## Risks / watch-fors
- If Bitcoin holds $62,534 and breaks above $79,125, a larger bounce scenario becomes active (though likely temporary).
- Bear market could extend into 2027 with prices lower than consensus expects.
- Long-term bull case depends on continued institutional adoption and sovereign debt deterioration playing out as projected.
