## Thesis
Neoclouds CoreWeave and Nebius are capturing massive hyperscaler demand by offering faster GPU deployment and superior utilization rates, but their growth is structurally dependent on debt-fueled capex that dwarfs operating cash flow. Nvidia's dual role as equity investor, GPU supplier, and demand backstop for unsold capacity creates a circular financing loop that raises durability questions. The model works while hyperscaler commitments hold, but rising interest rates and widening funding gaps are compounding risks.

## Key numbers
- Hyperscaler commitments: Microsoft ~$60B, Meta ~$62B, total >$145B including OpenAI/Anthropic
- CoreWeave FY2026E revenue: $12.6B; Nebius FY2026E: $3.4B — commitments are ~10x current sales
- CoreWeave Q1 revenue: $2.08B (+112% YoY); capex $7.7B; FCF -$4.71B; debt $24.86B (+16% QoQ)
- CoreWeave 2026 capex guidance midpoint: $33B vs. ~$8.7B estimated OCF — funding gap ~$17.3B
- CoreWeave interest expense: $536M in Q1 (25.8% of revenue, 46% of adj. EBITDA); guided to $690M in Q2
- Nebius Q1 revenue: $339M (+684% YoY); net cash $920M; 2026 capex midpoint $22.5B; additional funding needed ~$6.3B
- Nvidia backstop for CoreWeave unsold GPU capacity: initial value $6.3B, through April 2032
- Nvidia invested $2B each in CoreWeave and Nebius; prior stakes also disclosed in 13F filings
- 3-year Treasury rate rose from <3.6% to ~4.2% YTD, pressuring DDTL fixed-rate tranches

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Hyperscalers use neocloud contracts to shift capex off balance sheets as opex, a structural demand driver but also a bearish read on neocloud independence.
- CoreWeave's investment-grade DDTL rating depends on investment-grade customer contracts as collateral; DDTL 5.0 (non-IG customers) received a lower rating and higher rates — contract quality matters for funding cost.
- Only ~$1.26B of the $8.5B DDTL 4.0 facility has been drawn; remaining drawdowns will materially increase reported debt.
- Nebius has a meaningfully cleaner balance sheet than CoreWeave and a large undeployed ATM equity program (~$5B at ~$200/share).

## Risks / watch-fors
- Circular financing: Nvidia invests in neoclouds that immediately recycle capital back to Nvidia via GPU purchases; if Nvidia pulls back support, neocloud funding terms deteriorate.
- Rising rates increase borrowing costs on future facilities; interest expense already consuming ~27% of CoreWeave's projected Q2 revenue.
- Both companies have converted less than half their contracted power to active capacity; multi-year capex needs remain enormous relative to cash generation.
- Hyperscaler capex-to-opex shift could reverse if AI ROI disappoints, removing the primary demand driver.
