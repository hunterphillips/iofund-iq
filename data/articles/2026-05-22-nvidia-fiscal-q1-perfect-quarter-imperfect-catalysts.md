## Thesis
Nvidia delivered a near-flawless Q1 on every financial metric, but the forward catalyst picture is murkier than during Hopper/Blackwell cycles. Custom silicon encroachment on inference workloads, a shifting CPU-to-GPU attach rate, surging supply commitments, and a defensive segment restructuring all suggest the company's dominant position faces incremental headwinds even as the broader AI infrastructure market remains parabolic.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $81.6B (+85% YoY, +19.8% QoQ); beat own guidance of $78B
- Q2 guidance: $91B (~+95% YoY, +11.9% QoQ); ~$4B above consensus of ~$87B
- FY27 consensus estimate heading in: ~$373B; likely to move ~$10B+ higher post-beat
- Data Center revenue: $75.25B (+92% YoY, +21% QoQ); Networking sub-segment +199% YoY, +35% QoQ to $14.8B
- Hyperscale sub-segment: ~$37.9B (~50% of DC); ACIE sub-segment: ~$37.4B (+74% YoY, +31% QoQ)
- Adjusted gross margin: 75%; adjusted operating margin: ~65.9%
- Free cash flow: $48.6B (59.5% margin) vs. Apple's ~$28.7B
- Operating income: >$50B vs. Apple's ~$36B
- Supply-related commitments: $119B, up ~$90B YoY and ~$24B QoQ; ~$95B due in remainder of FY27
- Vera CPU standalone TAM cited: ~$200B; near-term CPU revenue visibility: ~$20B
- Morgan Stanley estimate: Rubin memory BOM ~$2M/rack, up ~435% from GB300's ~$374K

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- QoQ growth is decelerating: guided 11.5% for Q2 vs. 19–22% in prior three quarters; analyst estimates imply near-flat QoQ in September quarter before settling to ~6–7%.
- Rubin ramp timing is ambiguous: initial shipments in Q3, meaningful ramp in Q4, material revenue impact likely Q1 FY28 (~11 months out).
- Segment restructuring (Hyperscale vs. ACIE) appears defensive, pre-positioning investors for a future where hyperscaler budgets shift toward custom silicon.
- Management conflated inference "growth" with inference "market share" when directly questioned—a notable evasion on the market's central concern.
- Vera CPU opens a new ~$200B TAM and is additive, not cannibalistic per management, but the CPU-to-GPU ratio shift (from 1:8 toward 1:2 or 1:1) warrants monitoring.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Custom silicon (ASICs) projected to surpass GPU shipments at top-10 hyperscalers by 2028; inference market share erosion is the key unresolved question.
- Surging supply commitments ($119B) may reflect HBM/memory cost inflation rather than purely demand strength—memory could reach ~25% of Rubin BOM vs. <10% for GB300, creating margin pressure if pricing power is insufficient.
- Rubin timing slippage beyond Q1 FY28 would extend the current QoQ deceleration period.
- China Hopper revenue was zero in Q1; any further export restrictions could widen this gap.
