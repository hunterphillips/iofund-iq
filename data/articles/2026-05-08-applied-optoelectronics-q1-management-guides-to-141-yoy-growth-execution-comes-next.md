## Thesis
AAOI's Q1 was roughly in line with AI networking peers, but the real story is a dramatically raised full-year guide implying 141% YoY growth. The bull case rests on vertical integration in indium phosphide lasers giving AAOI a supply-chain edge competitors lack, enabling a massive 2H revenue ramp as 800G and 1.6T products scale from ~3% to ~80% of data center revenue mix.

## Key numbers
- Q1 revenue: $151.1M (+51% YoY, +13% QoQ); missed estimates by ~2%
- Full-year 2026 guide: >$1.1B vs. prior consensus ~$962M and 2025 actuals of $455.7M (~141% YoY implied)
- Q2 guide: $180M–$198M midpoint (~84% YoY, ~25% QoQ)
- Data center revenue: $81.4M (+154% YoY); 400G grew ~10x YoY; 800G only 5.6% of DC revenue today
- Target product mix in 12–18 months: 46% from 800G, 35% from 1.6T (vs. ~3% combined today)
- Transceiver capacity: ~100K units/month exiting Q1 → target >650K/month by end of 2026; >930K/month by end of 2027
- Laser fab expansion in Texas: ~350% capacity increase planned by end of 2027
- Adjusted gross margin: 29.2% (down 150 bps YoY); long-term target ~40%
- Free cash outflow: -$143.6M (-95% of revenue); capex +105% YoY to $58.2M
- Cash/short-term investments: $449.4M vs. debt of $206.5M; raised $382.5M via share issuance in Q1

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Revenue is heavily back-weighted; implied ~70% QoQ growth between Q3 and Q4 is an aggressive execution ask that the Street is unlikely to reward until it materializes.
- Vertical InP laser manufacturing is AAOI's key differentiator—competitors face 21–24 month lead times while AAOI claims ~1 year of inventory on hand and ongoing fab qualification.
- Shared production lines across 400G/800G/1.6T reduce capital requirements and accelerate time-to-market for the higher-margin product ramp.
- ELS/CPO opportunity is an emerging, longer-dated catalyst layered on top of the transceiver ramp.

## Risks / watch-fors
- InP capacity remains an industry-wide constraint; management commentary on supply security was notably vague, signaling execution risk.
- Any slip in equipment delivery or fab qualification timelines could force a guidance cut and sharp stock reaction given the stock's 350%+ YTD run.
- Gross margins remain below target at 29.2%; near-term data center mix is a headwind until higher-margin 800G/1.6T volumes dominate.
- Heavy cash burn (-$144M FCF in Q1) and dilutive equity issuance ($382.5M) are ongoing balance sheet watch items.
