## Thesis
Meta delivered its strongest top-line growth since late 2021, driven by AI-powered improvements to its ad stack that simultaneously lifted impressions, pricing, and per-user revenue. The company is transitioning its recommendation engine from pattern-recognition to LLM-based personalization, which should further strengthen the ads flywheel over the medium term. A subtle but notable shift in capex commentary—hinting at possible future reductions—is the key new variable to monitor.

## Key numbers
- Q1 2026 revenue: $56.3B, +33% YoY (vs. +16% YoY in Q1 2025)
- Ad revenue: $55B, +32.9% YoY
- Ad impressions growth: +19% YoY (accelerated ~14 pts YoY)
- Ad pricing growth: +12% YoY (up from +6% in Q4)
- ARPP: $15.66, +26.7% YoY (accelerated from +16.2% in Q4)
- Family DAP: 3.56B, +3.8% YoY
- Operating margin: 40.6% (vs. 41.5% year-ago)
- Q1 capex: $19.8B, +45% YoY; FY26 guide raised to $125–$145B midpoint ~$135B (+87% YoY)
- FCF: $12.4B (22% of revenue); FCF may turn negative as early as Q2
- Value optimization suite annualized run rate: >$20B, >2x YoY
- Q2 2026 revenue guide: $58–$61B (~+25% YoY at midpoint)

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- All three ad metrics (impressions, pricing, ARPP) accelerated simultaneously—a rare and bullish combination signaling genuine advertiser ROI improvement, not just volume.
- LLM-based recommendation overhaul is a multi-year buildout; 2026 is validation/architecture phase, with scaling in future years—engagement gains are real now but the bigger monetization uplift is still ahead.
- Reels time spent on Instagram up 10% and Facebook video watch time up 9% in Q1, driven by model improvements—engagement momentum is strong.

## Risks / watch-fors
- FCF likely turns negative in coming quarters given ~$115B remaining capex to deploy over three quarters vs. ~$32B quarterly operating cash flow.
- CFO's hint at potential future capex reductions is a meaningful tonal shift; watch whether this signals demand uncertainty or genuine capacity discipline.
- EU data-consent headwinds (less personalized ads) will weigh on European revenue with full-quarter impact starting Q2.
- Macro uncertainty (Iran conflict, softer US/Western Europe ad spending in Q1) embedded in Q2 guide range.
