## Thesis
Palantir delivered an exceptional Q1 with revenue accelerating to 85% YoY growth and margins hitting record levels, cementing its position as the most elite growth-plus-profitability story in enterprise software. However, softness in forward-looking bookings metrics—particularly TCV and RDV growth lagging revenue—warrants close monitoring given the stock's premium valuation.

## Key numbers
- Revenue: $1.633B, +85% YoY / +16% QoQ (11th consecutive quarter of acceleration)
- US Commercial revenue: $595M, +133% YoY / +18% QoQ; FY guidance raised to >$3.224B (~120%+ growth)
- US Government revenue: $687M, +84% YoY / +21% QoQ
- Adjusted operating margin: 60% (beat guidance by ~320 bps)
- Rule of 40: 145 (up from 127 in Q4)
- Adjusted FCF: $925M, 56.6% margin; FY guidance raised to $4.2–4.4B
- NRR: 150%, +11 points QoQ (largest sequential jump since late 2023)
- TCV booked: $2.41B, +61% YoY but down 43% QoQ; YoY growth decelerated sharply from 138%
- US Commercial TCV: $1.18B, +45% YoY but down ~9.5% QoQ
- GAAP EPS: $0.34 (beat by 33%); Adjusted EPS: $0.33 (+154% YoY)
- Cash/Treasuries: $8.03B; zero debt

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- Revenue and margin execution are essentially flawless; the company's FCF now exceeds what total revenue was one year prior.
- NRR at 150% has not yet absorbed the surge in large deals (>$10M) signed over the past several quarters—upside to 160%+ NRR is plausible through 2026–2027.
- Palantir's ontology-based data layer differentiates it structurally from legacy software facing AI disruption; cheaper inference amplifies demand for its agent operating system rather than commoditizing it.
- Analyst plans to rely on technicals for entry/exit decisions given valuation, while keeping fundamentals on a "tight leash."

## Risks / watch-fors
- TCV booked has been essentially flat-to-down for three quarters (ex-Q4's one-time international contracts), diverging from the upward trajectory seen in 2025—a rebound in Q2 is critical.
- RPO and RDV growing at only 9% and 6% QoQ respectively, both slower than revenue growth, signals potential future deceleration if not corrected.
- Higher mix of smaller deals ($1M–$5M, 65% of Q1 deals) may be compressing US Commercial TCV even as deal counts hit records.
- Valuation leaves no margin for error; any sustained softness in bookings metrics could trigger a meaningful re-rating lower.
