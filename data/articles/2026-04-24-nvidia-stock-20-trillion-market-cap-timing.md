## Thesis
The analyst maintains a $20 trillion Nvidia market cap target by 2030 (~310% upside), but believes most of that gain is back-half weighted (2028–2030) and driven by software/robotics rather than hardware. Near-term, three structural headwinds—CUDA moat erosion in inference, custom silicon share gains, and Rubin product delays—make other AI names more attractive for 2026 capital deployment.

## Key numbers
- Nvidia data center revenue: $194B vs. Intel's $17B (current)
- Nvidia supply commitments: surged ~90% QoQ to $95.2B in Q4
- Nvidia inventories: $21.4B, up >8% QoQ
- Nvidia P/E: 40.7x vs. 3-year median of 55.3x (trading ~26% below median)
- Expected FY2026 growth: >50% top- and bottom-line
- Analyst FY Jan-2028 revenue growth estimate: 30.1%
- Sensitivity analysis: at ~63% GPU market share, growth falls to ~15.6%
- Broadcom AI chip revenue target: $100B by 2027; AMD base case: ~$45B
- Implied 2027 market share: NVDA ~67%, down from ~90% in training era
- Custom silicon forecast (Counterpoint): 40M ASIC chips deployed 2024–2028; surpassing GPU shipments by 2028
- SK Hynix reportedly cutting HBM4 shipments to Nvidia by 20–30% in 2026
- I/O Fund YTD performance: ~+33% vs. QQQ barely positive

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- CUDA's moat is strongest in training, which is becoming a shrinking share of total compute; inference—the growth segment—favors open frameworks (vLLM, SGLang, ONNX) and custom silicon.
- Rubin delay (potentially two quarters, as Blackwell was) coincides with the window when custom silicon becomes most attractive, undermining Nvidia's product-roadmap as a secondary moat defense.
- Blackwell backfill likely prevents a 2026 revenue miss, but strategic narrative around hardware dominance is weakening.
- Analyst maintains ~5% NVDA position given attractive valuation and >50% earnings growth, but has rotated capital toward optical networking, photonics, and energy names.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Rubin delay could extend beyond one quarter (Blackwell precedent was two quarters); HBM4 validation and SK Hynix supply cuts are live risks.
- Custom silicon share gains may accelerate faster than consensus; MediaTek CoWoS capacity jumping from 20K to 150K wafers in 2027 signals a rapidly expanding competitor set.
- Emotional attachment to a long-term winner (held since ~$3.15 split-adjusted) can distort capital allocation discipline—analyst explicitly flags this behavioral risk.
