## Thesis
Palantir's dual challenge in 2026 is sustaining 50%+ US Commercial growth while justifying a stratospheric valuation. The analyst argues the company's competitive edge—handling incomplete datasets via its Ontology knowledge graph and AIP platform—is structurally differentiated from traditional BI tools, making the premium partially defensible but still demanding flawless execution.

## Key numbers
- Q3 revenue: $1.18B, +62.8% YoY (largest sequential acceleration since IPO; +14.8 pts QoQ)
- US Commercial revenue: $397M, +121% YoY (+28 pts acceleration); full-year outlook raised to >104% growth
- NRR: 134%, +6 pts sequentially, +27 pts over two years
- Q3 Rule of 40: 114% (vs. 94% prior quarter, 68% year-ago)
- Adjusted operating margin: 51% (first time above 50%); Q4 guided at 52%
- Adjusted EPS: $0.21, +110% YoY; FY26 consensus ~$1.01 (+39.5% YoY)
- Forward P/S: 67.5x; forward P/E: ~176.5x
- Full-year revenue guidance: ~$4.40B (~53.5% YoY growth, up from initial 30.9% guide)
- Adjusted FCF guidance: $1.9–$2.1B (~45.5% margin)
- Cash/securities: $6.4B; zero debt

## Decision-relevant takeaways
- AIP is the primary growth engine; US Commercial TCV surged 342% YoY to $1.31B, with deals >$5M up 5x YoY—pipeline quality is improving, not just volume.
- New products (AI FDEs, Hivemind, Edge Ontology) expand addressable markets into IoT/edge and accelerate deployment timelines dramatically (one migration cut from ~2 years to 5 days).
- Citi upgraded to Buy with a $235 target, citing an enterprise AI budget super-cycle and accelerating defense modernization spend as 2026 catalysts.

## Risks / watch-fors
- Valuation leaves almost no margin for error; at 67.5x forward P/S, any growth deceleration could trigger severe multiple compression.
- Billings growth decelerated to 48.8% YoY (from 53.5%) and RPO growth slowed to 66% YoY/+8% QoQ—early signals worth monitoring for demand softening.
- International Commercial remains weak (~$152M, +10% YoY), creating heavy dependence on the US market for the growth narrative.
